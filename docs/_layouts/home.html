Emotions in Reviews

Sometimes, it‚Äôs not just what reviewers say but how they say it. Reviews can carry powerful emotions, from joy to anger, and even despair! By analyzing the implied emotional state of the reviewer, we can find reviews where the frustration is palpable, whether it‚Äôs the anger of spending money on a dud or the sadness of a once-favorite being disappointing.

The previous approach of comparing high-rated and low-rated reviews works well for identifying more obvious criticisms, as low-rated reviews tend to be straightforward and direct about negative feedback. We use an emotion analysis model specifically on high-rated reviews because these reviews often contain subtle or mixed feedback. For example, a positive review might include some constructive criticism, but it can be harder to spot since the overall tone is positive. The emotion analysis model helps us identify these hidden emotions or critiques within the positive feedback, by analyzing sentence by sentence.

We used a machine learning tool to analyze the emotions in reviews. We could determine the overall emotion expressed in each review by giving the reviews to a model called Emotion English DistilRoBERTa-base. Reviews can have different sentiments, like anger, sadness, joy, and surprise. This tool helped us categorize these emotions in our dataset, which is useful for identifying if there‚Äôs a strong emotional element tied to specific types of beer.
